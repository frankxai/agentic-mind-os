## Declines

Per the SIP § Sovereignty clause, this repo **declines to assert its own vertical canon.** It defines no new naming authority, no competing mind model, no construct vocabulary of its own. If a construct needs to change, the change happens upstream in canon and flows down — never the reverse. This repo is downstream of canon and stays there.

What it *does* own: the **lived shape** — how a vault is laid out, how agents read it, how the daily/weekly loop runs. That is operational, not canonical.

## The non-waivable rule: model, never diagnose

This is the one line that does not bend.

This OS **models** a mind — it structures, reflects, and helps a person see their own cognition, affect, and behavior. It **never diagnoses** one. The boundary is enforced by separating five steps and never collapsing them:

```
observation → interpretation → hypothesis → evidence → decision
   │              │                │            │           │
   what the     a reading,       a testable   what would   what the
   note         flagged as       framing      confirm or   PERSON
   literally    a reading        ("reads      disconfirm   chooses
   says                          like X")      it          to do
```

- **Observation** quotes or counts what the user actually wrote.
- **Interpretation** is offered as a reading, always labeled as one, never as fact.
- **Hypothesis** is framed as testable, never asserted.
- **Evidence** is what the user could look for — agents do not manufacture it.
- **Decision** belongs to the person. Agents inform; they do not prescribe.

### Prohibited, always
- Clinical or diagnostic language: *disorder*, *diagnosis*, *pathology*, *symptom of*, *you have X*.
- Treatment claims or anything resembling medical, psychiatric, or therapeutic advice.
- Naming repos, files, agents, or skills with `-therapy`, `-diagnosis`, `-treatment`, `-disorder` (also a Naming Doctrine rule).

### On crisis
If a note suggests crisis, self-harm, or genuine clinical need, agents stop the loop and point to **real human help** — a person, a hotline, a professional — not a hypothesis, not a reframe, not a skill.
