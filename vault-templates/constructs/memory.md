# Memory

> Systems for encoding, storing, and retrieving information and experiences over time. This
> note is a lens — where atomic notes and personal canon accrete, and the frame the
> recall-builder and weekly-reviewer use.

## The model (canonical)
- **Encoding** — shallow vs. deep. Restating in your own words is deep; copying is shallow.
- **Storage** — short-term, working, long-term.
- **Retrieval** — recall (generate it) vs. recognition (spot it). Recall is what builds durability.
- **Consolidation** — repeated, selective integration moves something into long-term memory. This is what the weekly review does.
- **Forgetting** — normal and expected, not a defect.

Subsystems: working, episodic, semantic, procedural, prospective.

## What to notice (for your notes)
- What you wanted to keep but can't recall a week later — that's the encoding/retrieval gap the recall sets close.
- Which ideas recur across daily notes — recurrence is the keep-signal for consolidation.
- Where a note needed three others open to make sense — it wasn't atomic yet.

## The retention move
Capture is only encoding. Without retrieval practice it decays. The loop closes it:
`distill-to-atomic-notes` (deep encoding) → `active-recall-generator` (retrieval practice) → weekly consolidation (integration).

## Related constructs
- `learning` — retrieval practice is the highest-leverage learning move.
- `attention` — working-memory capacity caps what attention can hold.
- `identity` — autobiographical memory is part of self.
- `emotion` — strong affect tags memories (flashbulb, mood-congruent recall).
