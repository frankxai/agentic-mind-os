# Metacognition

> Thinking about your own thinking — monitoring, evaluating, and regulating your cognition.
> This note is a lens, not a test — a place to file what you notice, and the frame agents
> use to read your daily notes.

## What to notice (for your notes)
- What you learned about *how* you attend, decide, or learn (not just what happened).
- Where your sense of your own thinking was off (felt sure, was wrong; felt lost, knew it).
- Strategies you reached for, and whether they worked.

## Related constructs
- `learning` — knowing how you learn improves what you learn.
- `attention` — meta-attention is noticing where focus goes.
- `belief` — catching a belief is a metacognitive act.
- `decision-making` — reviewing a decision's process, not just its outcome.
