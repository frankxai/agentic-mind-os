# Attention

> The selective allocation of cognitive resources to specific stimuli, thoughts, or actions
> while filtering others. This note is a lens, not a test — a place to file what you notice
> about your own attention, and the frame agents use to read your daily notes.

## The model (canonical)
The shapes of attention worth telling apart:

- **Selective** — choosing one thing from many candidates.
- **Sustained / vigilance** — holding on one thing over time.
- **Divided** — splitting across more than one (usually costly).
- **Executive control** — keeping attention on your intention when something pulls it.
- **Orienting / alerting** — what grabs and what readies you.

When focus fails, it usually fails at one of these — naming which is the first move (`focus-coach` does this).

## What to notice (for your notes)
- What you keep returning to across days — that recurrence is a signal.
- What reliably pulls you off (a tab, a worry, a person, an open loop).
- Whether a bad focus day is a *selection* problem (too many candidates), a *sustaining* problem (can't hold), or a *control* problem (intention vs. pull).
- When attention is sharpest — time of day, after what, before what.

## Related constructs
- `memory` — working-memory capacity caps how much you can hold at once.
- `motivation` — you sustain attention on what you've got a reason to.
- `metacognition` — noticing your own attention *is* meta-attention.
- `emotion` — mood biases what you orient toward (attentional bias).
